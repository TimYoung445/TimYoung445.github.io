Artist Profiles

Leonardo Da Vinci

Born on April 15, 1452 and died on May 2, 1519

Leonardo da Vinci was a Renaissance polymath renowned for his masterpieces in art and science. Key works include the Mona Lisa, The Last Supper, and the Vitruvian Man. His extensive studies in anatomy, engineering, and hydraulics reflect his innovative thinking. Da Vinci's insatiable curiosity and creativity make him one of history's most influential figures.

Mona Lisa (c. 1503-1506), The Last Supper (1495-1498), Vitruvian Man (c. 1490), Saint John the Baptist (c. 1513-1516)

Vincent Van Gogh

Born on March 30, 1853 and died on July 29, 1890

Vincent van Gogh was a Dutch post-impressionist painter known for his vivid colors and emotional depth. Born in 1853, he created over 2,100 artworks, including iconic pieces like Starry Night, Sunflowers, and The Bedroom. Despite struggling with mental health issues and only gaining fame after his death in 1890, his expressive brushwork and bold use of color have had a lasting impact on modern art.

Starry Night (1889), Sunflowers (series, 1888), The Bedroom (1888), The Café Terrace on the Place du Forum, Arles, at Night (1888), Irises (1889)

Pablo Picasso

Born on October 25, 1881 and died on April 8, 1973

Pablo Picasso was a Spanish painter and sculptor, co-founder of the Cubist movement and one of the most influential artists of the 20th century. Born on October 25, 1881, he is known for his diverse styles and prolific output, with notable works including Les Demoiselles d'Avignon and Guernica. Picasso's innovative techniques and exploration of form and color have left a lasting legacy in modern art.

Les Demoiselles d'Avignon (1907), Guernica (1937), The Weeping Woman (1937), The Old Guitarist (1903-1904)

Claude Monet

Date of Birth and Death

Claude Monet was a French painter and a key figure in the Impressionist movement, celebrated for his groundbreaking use of color and light. Born on November 14, 1840, he is best known for his series of landscapes, such as Water Lilies and Impression, Sunrise, which capture fleeting moments and the beauty of nature through innovative brushwork.

Impression, Sunrise (1872), Water Lilies (series, 1896-1926), Woman with a Parasol (1875), The Japanese Bridge (1899)

Johannes Vermeer

Born in October 1632 and died on December 15, 1675

Johannes Vermeer was a Dutch painter renowned for his exquisite use of light and color, capturing intimate domestic scenes. Born around October 1632, he is best known for masterpieces such as Girl with a Pearl Earring and The Milkmaid. Vermeer’s meticulous attention to detail and innovative composition have made him a pivotal figure in Baroque art.

Girl with a Pearl Earring (c. 1665), The Milkmaid (c. 1658), View of Delft (c. 1660-1661), The Art of Painting (c. 1666-1668)

Edvard Munch

Born on December 12, 1863 and died on January 23, 1944

Edvard Munch was a Norwegian painter and printmaker, best known for his expressionist works that explore themes of love, anxiety, and existential dread. Born on December 12, 1863, he gained fame with his iconic piece The Scream, which captures profound emotional turmoil. Munch’s innovative use of color and form profoundly influenced modern art.

The Scream (1893), The Madonna (1894-1895), The Dance of Life (1899-1900), The Sick Child (1885-1886)

Salvador Dali

Born on May 11, 1904 and died on January 23, 1989

Salvador Dalí was a Spanish surrealist artist known for his eccentric personality and imaginative works that explore dreams, the subconscious, and the bizarre. Born on May 11, 1904, he is best known for iconic pieces like The Persistence of Memory, which features melting clocks. Dalí's unique style and flamboyant persona made him a key figure in 20th-century art.

The Persistence of Memory (1931), The Elephants (1948), The Sacrament of the Last Supper (1955), The Great Masturbator (1929)

Andy Warhol

Born on August 6, 1928 and died on February 22, 1987

Andy Warhol was an American artist and leading figure in the Pop Art movement, known for his bold use of commercial imagery and innovative techniques. Born on August 6, 1928, he famously blurred the lines between high art and consumer culture. Iconic works like Campbell's Soup Cans and Marilyn Diptych reflect his fascination with mass production and celebrity.

Campbell's Soup Cans (1962), Marilyn Diptych (1962), Brillo Boxes (1964), Silver Clouds (1966)

René François Ghislain Magritte

Born on November 21, 1898 and died on August 15, 1967

René Magritte was a Belgian surrealist painter known for his thought-provoking and imaginative works that challenge perceptions of reality. Born on November 21, 1898, he employed everyday objects in unusual contexts, creating a sense of mystery. Iconic pieces like The Son of Man and The Treachery of Images exemplify his exploration of visual language and symbolism.

The Son of Man (1964), The Treachery of Images (Ceci n'est pas une pipe) (1928-1929), The Lovers (1928), The Human Condition (1933)

Henri Matisse

Born on December 31, 1869 and died on November 3, 1954

Henri Matisse was a French painter and sculptor, known for his vibrant color palettes and innovative use of form. Born on December 31, 1869, he was a leading figure in the Fauvism movement, which emphasized bold, expressive colors. Matisse's works, such as The Dance and Woman with a Hat, showcase his mastery of color and composition, significantly influencing modern art.

The Dance (1910), Woman with a Hat (1905), The Joy of Life (Le Bonheur de Vivre) (1905-1906), The Snail (1953)
